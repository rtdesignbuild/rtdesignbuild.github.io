Mission Statement

As inherited by our biblical ancestors, we were all created by our Almighty Creator according to His image. God intended us to be naked and unashamed, but fallen angels have spread lies about nudity, making us slaves of our inherited sins, immoralities, and sexual impurities.

Even though we are living in a fallen world with insurmountable sins, it's still within Christian holy responsibility to preserve the original innocence of Garden of Eden. However difficult or seemingly impossible the reality presents itself, we Christians as naturists/nudists should try our very best to present ourselves as God intended, of course, we realize we must do so only when the situations deemed appropriate, with the divine inspiration from the Word of God, along with our faithful prayer granting us the ability to discern the truth from lies.


Divine Inspiration From God's Word

(NKJV - New King James Version)

[Matthew 6:33]:
But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you.

[James 1:5]:
If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all liberally and without reproach, and it will be given to him.

[Romans 12:2]:
And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind, that you may prove what is that good and acceptable and perfect will of God.

[Matthew 7:1-2]:
“Judge not, that you be not judged. For with what judgment you judge, you will be judged; and with the measure you use, it will be measured back to you.”

[Ephesians 5:8-13]:
For you were once darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Walk as children of light (for the fruit of the Spirit is in all goodness, righteousness, and truth), finding out what is acceptable to the Lord. And have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather expose them. For it is shameful even to speak of those things which are done by them in secret. But all things that are exposed are made manifest by the light, for whatever makes manifest is light.

[1 Corinthians 2:13-15]:
These things we also speak, not in words which man’s wisdom teaches but which the Holy Spirit teaches, comparing spiritual things with spiritual. But the natural man does not receive the things of the Spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him; nor can he know them, because they are spiritually discerned. But he who is spiritual judges all things, yet he himself is rightly judged by no one.


God's Original Perfect Divine Plan

It would be a downright insult to God, more so as a disrespect and an apparent blasphemy against God if we denied His original divine and perfect plan in designing us as “naked and unashamed”. As stated in the Book of Genesis 2:25, “And they were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed” (NKJV - New King James Version).

The “Gospel of Thomas” also cited that during the great rapture in the second coming of Christ, Jesus expects us to be simply nude and bare naked, with child-like manner awaiting for the return of our Heavenly Father. This revelation is well echoed in the following Divine Biblical verses:

(NKJV - New King James Version)

[1 John 2:28]:
“And now, little children, abide in Him, that when He appears, we may have confidence and not be ashamed before Him at His coming.”

Also in [Matthew 19:14]:
Jesus said, “Let the little children come to Me, and do not forbid them; for of such is the kingdom of heaven.”


Reaching Out With Modesty

Moreover, Holy Scripture has taught us to be modest in every aspect of our life. Hence, we reserve the right to limit our exposure to nudity when circumstances deemed inappropriate.

(NKJV - New King James Version)

[1 Corinthians 6:19-20]:
Or do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you are not your own? For you were bought at a price; therefore glorify God in your body and in your spirit, which are God’s.

[1 Corinthians 12:23]:
And those members of the body which we think to be less honorable, on these we bestow greater honor; and our unpresentable parts have greater modesty.


Our Objectives with Divine Conviction

Our main goal is to reach out to naturist and nudist communities, and glorify God through non-conventional venues of spreading the Gospel in the Almighty name of Christ Jesus.

We are also accepting “Prayer Request” as a way of strengthening our faith. Upholding our strong value for being bare naked as God intended. Resisting the temptation of worldly sins. Defying the opposition with resilience. Walk with faith without fear or shame, but rather, walk and move forward with confidence according to God's divine plan.

(NKJV - New King James Version)

[2 Corinthians 5:6-8]:
So we are always confident, knowing that while we are at home in the body we are absent from the Lord. For we walk by faith, not by sight. We are confident, yes, well pleased rather to be absent from the body and to be present with the Lord.


Stay Resilience with Strong Faith

We will remain steadfast in pursuing God's calling to fulfill His divine purposes for Christian naturists. As the following verses in the Holy Scripture attested:

(NKJV - New King James Version)

[Psalm 23:4-6]:
Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil; For You are with me; Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me. You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; You anoint my head with oil; My cup runs over. Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life; And I will dwell in the house of the Lord Forever.

[Romans 8:28]:
And we know that all things work together for good to those who love God, to those who are the called according to His purpose.

[1 Timothy 6:12]:
Fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on eternal life, to which you were also called and have confessed the good confession in the presence of many witnesses.

[Ephesians 6:12]:
For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this age, against spiritual hosts of wickedness in the heavenly places.

[Romans 8:31]:
What then shall we say to these things? If God is for us, who can be against us?

[1 John 1:9]:
If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.

[Ephesians 2:10]:
For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand that we should walk in them.

[Psalm 91:1]:
He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High Shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty.

[Isaiah 43:2]:
